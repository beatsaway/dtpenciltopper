Evaluation of Prototype

Prototyping means creating a model to test a concept or process. It can be made from cheap materials like paper, cardboard, or styrofoam. This helps test ideas without wasting materials or adding costs.

During the prototyping lesson for our pencil topper design, we tested:

  • If the gap fits securely on a pencil.
  • If the shape is too complex to cut.
  • If the size is practical.
  • If it looks good on a pencil.

Homework: Answer the evaluation questions in your DT book using full sentences.

* These images are just examples and do not include all student prototypes. It’s okay if your design is not shown here. Please complete the evaluation based on your own prototype.

Evaluation Questions

  1. What did you do in the lesson?
    Describe the steps to make our prototype.
  2. What was easy or difficult?
    Reflect on your challenges and how you solved them.
  3. What tools did you use?
    How to ensure safety?
  4. What would you do differently?
    Think about improvements you’d make next time.
  5. What should you remember?
    Identify key considerations for making your pencil topper.
